## Published figure candidates

### Conditioned hallucinations and HGF modeling

The most relevant research graph candidate is from Powers, Mathys, and Corlett’s conditioned hallucination study. Their HGF modeling figure is useful because it directly visualizes the relationship among low-level perceptual belief, audio-visual association, volatility, and prior weighting relative to sensory evidence.

Use path: cite directly, then create an original adapted diagram unless reuse terms are explicitly reviewed.

### Protein misfolding and chaperone intervention

The molecular chaperone review includes useful conceptual figure ideas: amyloid formation pathways, prion-like propagation, and chaperone mechanisms. These should be used as scientific inspiration for original diagrams rather than copied directly unless license terms are reviewed.

## Research hygiene

- Prefer primary, peer-reviewed, or institutionally grounded sources.
- Do not over-universalize Indigenous land-based knowledge; keep specificity, community context, and authorship visible.
- Treat protein misfolding and reward-loop material as metaphors unless making a directly supported scientific claim.
